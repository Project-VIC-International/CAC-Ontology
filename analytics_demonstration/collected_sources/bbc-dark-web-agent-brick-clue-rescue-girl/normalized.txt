Title: How dark web agent spotted bedroom wall clue to rescue girl from years of harm
Publisher: BBC News / BBC Eye Investigations
Byline (as stated): Sam Piranty

Captured: 2026-02-11
Content warning: includes details about child sexual abuse (no CSAM content stored)

An online investigator (Greg Squire) worked to rescue an abused girl his team named “Lucy” after images were shared on the dark web.
The abuser attempted to avoid identification by cropping/altering images to remove identifying features; investigators had difficulty identifying who or where the child was.
Investigators inferred a broad region (North America) from light sockets and electrical outlets visible in images.
Investigators analyzed visual details in the child’s bedroom (bedspread, outfits, toys) seeking identifying clues.
Investigators discovered a sofa shown in images was sold regionally, narrowing a customer base but still leaving a large candidate set.
Investigators used an exposed brick wall visible in images as a clue, and searched for brick-industry expertise (including contacting the Brick Industry Association).
A brick expert (John Harp) identified the brick type (“Flaming Alamo”) and explained that bricks are heavy and usually do not travel far, enabling geographic narrowing near the brick factory.
Investigators combined the regional sofa customer list with the brick factory proximity constraint, then searched social media and found a photo of Lucy with an adult who appeared close to her.
Investigators used that lead to identify an address and associated addresses; they avoided door-to-door checks to reduce risk of tipping off the suspect.
Investigators sent candidate house photos to the brick expert to assess whether homes were likely built during the relevant period, narrowing to one address.
Investigators confirmed household occupants via records and school information and determined Lucy lived with her mother’s boyfriend, a convicted sex offender.
Local agents arrested the offender; he was later sentenced to more than 70 years in jail; Lucy was rescued.
The reporting includes investigator occupational stress impacts: increased alcohol use, marriage breakdown, and suicidal thoughts; a colleague encouraged help-seeking.
Later, the investigator met Lucy as an adult; Lucy described increased stability and support and said she had been praying for the abuse to end.
