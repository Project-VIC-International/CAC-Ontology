Source URL: https://ncptf.org/westchester-missing-child-rescue-operation-results-in-successful-location-of-44-children-and-teens/
For Immediate Release: 2026-02-13

1) “Westchester Missing Child Rescue Operation” coordinated by New York State DCJS Missing Persons Clearinghouse with OCFS and NCPTF; >70 local/state/federal, nonprofit, and private partners.
2) Reported outcome: 43 children/teens reported missing in Westchester County located through the operation (article also notes “46… as of 12 p.m. Saturday, Feb 14, 2026,” with cases still in progress).
3) Operation duration: three-day operation concluding Thursday; activities included exploring new leads, reviewing case notes, and leveraging technology to locate children at risk of endangerment/exploitation/harm.
4) Recovered children ages at report: 8–17 years old.
5) OCFS coordinated with nonprofits and victim assistance programs to support recovered children and prevent future missing episodes.
6) NY statewide context (2025): 10,629 children <18 reported missing to police; 94% reported as runaways; 1,079 active missing children cases at end of 2025.
7) Quotes emphasize “information sharing,” “pursue new leads,” and “connect children with the services they need.”
8) This was described as the third NYS Missing Child Operation (MCO); prior operations: Erie County (47 located) and Capital Region (71 located).
9) Named Westchester partners include multiple local police departments, District Attorney’s Office, Department of Social Services, Probation Department, and a Real Time Crime Center.
10) Federal/national partners named: FBI, NCMEC, U.S. Marshals Service; national nonprofit and private sector partners provided investigative/strategic/technology support.
11) Material support: 914 Cares embedded in the command center providing clothing/hygiene/winter items and other essentials for children located.
12) Technology enablement: Verizon Frontline provided secure Wi‑Fi for the command center; multiple private-sector partners expedited urgent support requests to locate endangered children.
